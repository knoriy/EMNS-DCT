Open Empathic Data Annotation Manual

Welcome to our data annotation team! We are thrilled to have you on board.

Your invaluable annotations will contribute to the creation of open-source datasets that are aimed at enhancing the emotional intelligence of AI models. By providing these annotations, you will play a crucial role in enabling researchers worldwide to equip AI systems with empathy, compassion, and a deep understanding of emotions.

Our most important guideline is to trust your intuition and avoid overthinking when performing annotations. Your unique insights and instincts are vital for our research and analysis.

Let's get started!

The Task

Rating Arousal and Valence:

Arousal:
Arousal measures the level of intensity or activation of the speaker's emotions. It indicates how energetic, stimulated, or excited the speaker appears. Here are some guidelines to help you rate arousal (from 0 to 10):

  • Low Arousal: The speaker sounds calm, relaxed, or even bored.
  • Medium Arousal: The speaker appears moderately engaged, showing some level of interest or excitement.
  • High Arousal: The speaker displays intense emotions, enthusiasm, or excitement.

Valence:
Valence measures the speaker's emotional state. It indicates whether the speaker is experiencing positive or negative emotions. Here are some guidelines to help you rate valence (from -5 to +5):

  • Negative Valence: The speaker displays sadness, anger, frustration, or other negative emotions.
  • Neutral Valence: The speaker appears emotionally neutral or displays a lack of strong positive or negative emotions.
  • Positive Valence: The speaker expresses joy, happiness, satisfaction, or other positive emotions.

Audio Quality:

Excellent:
This label represents the highest level of quality. It indicates that the audio recording is clear, crisp, and free from any distortions, background noise, or technical issues.

Good:
This label signifies a high-quality audio recording with minor imperfections that do not significantly affect the overall listening experience. There may be slight background noise or minor audio artifacts, but they are not distracting.

Average:
This label suggests a moderately satisfactory audio recording. It may have noticeable imperfections, such as intermittent background noise, occasional dropouts, or minor distortions. The recording is still intelligible, but there is room for improvement.

Poor:
This label indicates a low-quality audio recording that is marred by significant issues. It may have consistent background noise, noticeable distortions, frequent dropouts, or muffled sound. The recording may be difficult to understand or listen to without effort.

Unusable:
This label is reserved for audio recordings that are of such poor quality that they cannot be effectively used or understood. The recording may be heavily distorted, filled with excessive background noise, or plagued by continuous dropouts, making it impossible to extract meaningful information.

Describing the Speaker's Feelings:

In this step, you will describe the feelings the speaker seems to experience during the audio clip. Use your own words to convey the emotions, aiming for a minimum of 10 words. We encourage you to use rich vocabulary to capture the nuances of the speaker's emotions. While we provide dropdown menus of emotional words for your convenience, feel free to include other appropriate words that you believe suit the task.

Choose words that accurately portray the speaker's emotional state within the given audio clip. Feel free to use multiple words if necessary to capture the complexity of emotions.
